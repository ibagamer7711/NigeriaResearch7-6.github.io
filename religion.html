Religion:



Nigeria is home to some of the world's largest Muslim and Christian population. The country is roughly divided between Muslims and Christians with Muslims living in the north and Christians living more in the south. There is also a small percentage of indigenous religions relating to the Igbo and the Yoruba tribe. In Nigeria, the percentage of Muslims is slightly higher than Christians. Which is due to lower fertility rates in the South. Most Christians are protestants and a quarter of them are Catholic. According to the 2019 World Fact Book by the CIA, the religious population of Nigeria is 53.5% Muslims, 45.9% Christians, and 0.6% as other indigenous religions.
